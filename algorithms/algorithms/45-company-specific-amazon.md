# Company-Specific: Amazon - Interview Guide

## Overview
Amazon focuses on Leadership Principles, system design, and practical problem-solving.

---

## Interview Format
- **Phone Screen**: 1 hour, 1-2 coding problems
- **Onsite**: 4-5 rounds (3-4 coding, 1 behavioral, 1 system design)
- **Bar Raiser**: One interviewer focuses on culture fit

---

## Leadership Principles (Key for Amazon)
1. **Customer Obsession**
2. **Ownership**
3. **Invent and Simplify**
4. **Learn and Be Curious**
5. **Hire and Develop the Best**
6. **Insist on Highest Standards**
7. **Think Big**
8. **Bias for Action**
9. **Frugality**
10. **Earn Trust**
11. **Dive Deep**
12. **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit**
13. **Deliver Results**

---

## Frequently Asked Problems
1. **Two Sum** - Arrays
2. **LRU Cache** - Design
3. **Merge K Sorted Lists** - Heaps
4. **Word Ladder** - BFS
5. **Number of Islands** - DFS/BFS
6. **Top K Frequent Elements** - Heaps
7. **Trapping Rain Water** - Two pointers
8. **Longest Palindromic Substring** - DP
9. **Copy List with Random Pointer** - Linked list
10. **Serialize/Deserialize Binary Tree** - Trees

---

## System Design Topics
- **Scalability**: Design for millions of users
- **Availability**: 99.99% uptime
- **Consistency**: CAP theorem
- **Load Balancing**: Distribute traffic
- **Caching**: Redis, Memcached
- **Databases**: SQL vs NoSQL

---

## Tips
- Use STAR method for behavioral questions
- Link answers to Leadership Principles
- Show ownership and bias for action
- Discuss trade-offs in system design
- Practice live coding with interviewer interaction
